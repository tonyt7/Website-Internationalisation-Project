About Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a stunning tropical paradise located in southern India. With its lush green landscapes, serene backwaters, pristine beaches, and rich cultural heritage, Kerala offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness that entices travelers from all over the world. From the serene houseboat cruises in the backwaters to the rejuvenating Ayurveda treatments, from the enchanting tea gardens of Munnar to the majestic waterfalls of Athirappally, Kerala is a traveler's delight. Immerse yourself in the warm hospitality of the locals, savor the flavors of traditional Kerala cuisine, and explore the unique wildlife sanctuaries and historic temples that dot the landscape. Kerala is a perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful and rejuvenating escape amidst nature's splendor.



Why called God's Own Country

Kerala is often referred to as "God's Own Country" due to its breathtaking natural beauty, diverse cultural heritage, and unique charm that is believed to be bestowed by the gods themselves. The title "God's Own Country" originated from a tourism campaign launched by the Government of Kerala in the 1980s to promote tourism in the region. It was used to capture the essence of Kerala's pristine backwaters, palm-fringed beaches, lush green landscapes, serene hill stations, and rich cultural heritage, which are often compared to a heavenly paradise on earth. The nickname "God's Own Country" reflects the picturesque landscapes, abundant natural resources, and the warm hospitality of the people of Kerala, making it a favored destination for tourists seeking a tranquil and rejuvenating escape
